MS Dhoni

Biography

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, known as MS Dhoni, was born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand. His father, Pan Singh, was a junior manager at MECON, a Ministry of Steel PSU, and his mother, Devaki Devi, works from home.

Dhoni's family has a cricketing background. His father was a wicketkeeper for the Bihar state team for several seasons, and his grandfather, Nand Kishore Singh, played first-class cricket for Bihar and East Zone.

Dhoni attended school in Shyamali, near Ranchi, Jharkhand. He was an exemplary student-athlete, showing a preference for badminton and football. He was the goalkeeper for his school’s football team.

From 1995 to 1998, his football coach sent him to a local cricket club to be a wicketkeeper, which was an accidental discovery of his talent. He became the regular wicketkeeper for the Commando cricket club during that period.

From 1997–98, Dhoni made it to the Vinoo Mankad Trophy Under-16 Championship team. After completing 10th grade, he began to take cricket seriously.

Achievements

  • Led India to victory in the 2007 ICC World Twenty20.
  • Won the 2010 and 2016 Asia Cups.
  • Led India to win the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup.
  • Victory in the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy.
  • Known for his exceptional wicketkeeping skills and finishing abilities.
